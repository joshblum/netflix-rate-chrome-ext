Originally titled “First Last Kiss” Hitch is a surprisingly original and hilarious look at love. Will Smith is Alex Hitchens a ladies man and date doctor of urban myth proportions. Enter the beautiful Eva Mendes (Out of Time) as Sara; a gossip columnist and Hitch’s polar opposite. It’s her job to di...read moreg up dirt and break people apart. When these two meet they are instantly attracted to one another but falling in love goes against everything Sarah believes in. The longer Hitch and Sarah spend with one another the quicker there dates dissolve into a monumental disaster. In the interim Hitch gives advice to Albert played by Kevin James (King of Queens) an insecure financial advisor in love with his rich socialite client Allegra Cole. Just watching Albert’s nervous excitement as Hitch lays down the dating rules and dance moves, both of which Albert refuses to obey, is entertaining enough. Though the characters in “Hitch” always seems like comedic silhouettes of real people they are still human enough to care about and funny enough to laugh at. It’s obvious “Hitch” was a true labor of love for director Andy Tennant (Sweet Home Alabama), a sentiment truly expressed by the actors and felt by the audience.

Like the recent summer flick "Harold and Kumar go to Whitecastle," this is one of those few gems capable of helping us take ourselves a little less seriously, laugh at how silly we are and lighten up about the big things: life, love, desire. As with most any comedy -- romantic or otherwise -- "Hitch...read more" boats its share of condescendingly predictable twists and turns, and nearly derails toward the end in a desperate attempt at tying all loose knots with flowery ribbons. I was surprised at just how consistently entertaining this film was without giving in to the temptations that most films like this can't resist. For the first 80% of the film, we are not always told how to think about what we're seeing or how to feel about the characters and their decisions. Not all the work is done for us. We are allowed to laugh without being told why we're laughing, and that's an achievement most romantic comedies just can't claim. "Hitch" makes for a fantastic date movie and might even give some cynics like myself a glimpse of the bright side. I recommend it.

More humorous than romantic (as the romance side is a bit cheesy and corny) but essentially this film isn't your ordinary flick you'd expected when you first watch it. It's almost a satirical piece on love and modern dating/romance. Despite the great advice from Hitch (Will Smith), all of his love a...read moredvice are essentially tools that can help you open the door. Hitch's moves are only meant to sweep a woman off their feet (like a one-trick-pony or best first impressions). Since there are great similarities in a lot of relationships especially when they first start out (the nervousness, puppy dog love stage, first fight, etc etc), Hitch is able to guide his clients through this stage.Unbeknownst even Hitch, however, it's not necessarily his advice that makes relationships successful. Both Will's and Eva's characters are right in the sense that Will believes he can help people meet their "soulmates" and Eva believes it's wrong to use a love doctor to spark a relationship.Essentially, despite being a pretty decent romantic comedy, both Will and Eva's character as well as all the supporting characters (who were great BTW; maybe better than the stars) learn throughout the movie that love isn't something that is black and white. It can't be described or it can't be taught (evident in the socialites' adoration for Kevin James' character's nervousness and awkwardness combined with his unwillingness to let this opportunity slide *first kiss*).Hitch does nothing to show he really knows what love is; however he DOES seem to know a lot about body language and what a woman is thinking just by how they are. This film also follows Will in figuring out what that word really means.
